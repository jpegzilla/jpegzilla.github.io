I updated my website!

2022 02 22

I updated my website today! after two years of veeeeerry slow progress, I finally managed to finish it. I finished up the final designs yesterday, and I blazed through localization and configuration stuff today! I'm very proud of it, even though it took so long. also, isn't it weird that after two years, I finished it all on 2022 02 22? that's cool.

a lot has happened in the last two years — I moved to maryland, got engaged to my favorite person in the world, got pretty good at cooking, and a lot more, of course. it's been a fun ride up till now!

also, thanks for reading. that's really all I had to say — go check it out!! I also put some minor development notes below if you're interested.

some notes I took during the process.

I put a lot of tiny details into this iteration of my website. I want the web to be more fun and interactive, so I put a bunch of little fun things on my website like sprinkles on a cake. I'll keep adding easter eggs over time, as well!

variants and randomness

one of the major concepts of my website has always been that of variants. there are several components of my website that change based on user preference, window size, and more! there are also things that randomly change every time the site is reloaded, like the loading screen text and the little text underneath my wordmark in the upper right-hand corner.

I spent a lot of time picking words and phrases to randomly generate interesting combinations for that wordmark flavor text. sometimes you get a really cool one, like 'elemental purity' or 'heavenly monuments', or 'dragon's chrysalis'. some of them are strange, and some are just meh. that's the beautiful power of random chance!

easter eggs

aside from the obvious things like randomized text in some places, there are a lot of other easter eggs that I put a ton of effort into over the past few months. there may or may not be literal cheat codes for this website that you can enter on your keyboard.

music

another fun thing I did was add a comment at the top of a lot of the source code files, showing what music I was listening to at the time that I wrote the code. it could be fun to look through some if you have some time to waste. example here! or here!
